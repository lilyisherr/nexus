Getting Started

5 articles

Nexus uses Google sign-in, so there's no password to set up or remember.

  1. Click Get Started on the homepage or go to the login page.
  2. Click Sign in with Google.
  3. Pick the Google account connected to your YouTube channel.
  4. Approve the permissions Nexus asks for (public YouTube data and basic profile info).
  5. Done. You land on your dashboard.
Nexus only asks for access to public YouTube data and your basic Google profile. Your Google password is never seen or stored by Nexus.

Once you're logged in, add channels from your dashboard.

  1. Go to your Dashboard.
  2. Click Add Channel.
  3. Paste your YouTube Channel ID (the string starting with UC).
  4. Click Add. Nexus pulls in your channel name, thumbnail, subscriber count, views, and video count.

You can add multiple channels. Each one gets its own analytics and bot settings, completely independent from the others.

Not sure where to find your Channel ID? Go to YouTube, click your profile picture, then Settings > Advanced settings. The Channel ID is listed there.

The dashboard is where you see all your connected channels at a glance. Each channel card shows:

  • Channel thumbnail and name
  • Subscriber count
  • Total views across all videos
  • Video count
  • Last synced timestamp

Click any channel card to open its detailed analytics page with growth data and video performance.

The Videos page shows all uploaded videos for a channel, laid out similar to how YouTube itself displays them. Each video card includes:

  • Thumbnail, title, and upload date
  • View count, like count, and comment count
  • Video duration

You can sync your latest videos by clicking the Sync Videos button on the channel page. Nexus fetches the data from YouTube's API, so it stays up to date.

Only relevant if you're running your own instance of Nexus. Regular users don't need to do this.

  1. Go to the Google Cloud Console.
  2. Create a new project or select an existing one.
  3. Navigate to APIs & Services > Credentials.
  4. Click Create Credentials > OAuth client ID.
  5. Select Web application.
  6. Add your domain under Authorized JavaScript origins.
  7. Add https://yourdomain.com/auth/callback under Authorized redirect URIs.
  8. Copy the Client ID and Client Secret into your environment variables: GOOGLE_CLIENT_ID and GOOGLE_CLIENT_SECRET.
Getting a "redirect_uri_mismatch" error? The redirect URI in Google Cloud Console has to exactly match your site URL, including https:// and the /auth/callback path.

Bot Setup

5 articles

This is the one step you have to do before the bot can work. YouTube requires that any account posting in your live chat has moderator permissions. Without it, the bot can read messages but can't respond, delete spam, or do anything useful.

  1. Open YouTube Studio and sign in with the account that owns your channel.
  2. Click Settings in the left sidebar (gear icon near the bottom).
  3. Click Community on the left side of the Settings popup.
  4. Under Automated Filters, find the Moderators section.
  5. Type nexusbetabot in the moderator field and select the matching account.
  6. Click Save.

You only need to do this once per channel. The bot stays as a moderator until you remove it.

If nexusbetabot doesn't show up in the search, try searching for the full YouTube channel URL instead. Make sure you're in the Community tab, not General.

After adding nexusbetabot as a moderator on YouTube:

  1. Go to your Dashboard and click on a channel.
  2. Click the Settings button.
  3. Toggle Bot Enabled to on.
  4. Set your chat prefix (default is !), response delay, and join message if you want one.
  5. Save your settings.

The bot joins your live chat automatically when you go live. It runs on its own, so you don't need to keep any page open.

Adding nexusbetabot as a moderator gives it the same permissions as any human moderator:

  • Send messages in live chat (command responses, join message)
  • Delete messages that break your moderation rules
  • Temporarily hide users who keep breaking rules

Moderators cannot:

  • Upload, edit, or delete your videos
  • Change channel settings, branding, or monetization
  • Access your YouTube Studio or revenue data
  • Permanently ban users (only channel owners can do that)

You can remove nexusbetabot anytime from YouTube Studio > Settings > Community.

If the bot is enabled but not doing anything in your chat, go through this list:

  1. Is nexusbetabot added as a moderator? Most common issue. Check YouTube Studio > Settings > Community.
  2. Is the bot toggled on in Nexus? Go to channel settings and check the Bot Enabled switch.
  3. Are you actually live? The bot only works during live streams. It doesn't appear in Premieres or regular video comments.
  4. Did you save your settings? Changes don't apply until you hit Save.
  5. Give it a minute. There can be a short delay between going live and the bot connecting.

If none of that fixes it, try removing nexusbetabot as a moderator in YouTube Studio, save, then add it back. Still stuck? Use the contact page to let me know.

Each channel has its own bot settings. Here's what each one does:

  • Chat Prefix - The character that triggers commands. Default is !, so commands look like !discord or !socials.
  • Response Delay - How many seconds the bot waits before replying. Keeps it from feeling too instant and robotic. Default is 2 seconds.
  • Join Message - A message the bot sends when it first connects to your chat. Leave it blank if you don't want one.
  • Welcome Message - A greeting sent to new viewers when they first chat. Uses {user} as a placeholder for the viewer's name.
All settings are per-channel. If you manage multiple channels, each one can have completely different rules.

Moderation & Filters

3 articles

Nexus has three built-in chat filters. Each one can be toggled on or off independently.

Spam Filter

  • Catches repetitive spam messages
  • Max repeat characters limits stuff like "AAAAAA" or "!!!!!!"
  • Max message length blocks excessively long messages

Link Filter

  • Blocks all links in chat by default
  • Add trusted domains to the whitelist to let specific links through (your website, Discord invite, etc.)
  • Enter one domain per line: youtube.com, discord.gg

Caps Filter

  • Removes messages that are mostly ALL CAPS
  • Max caps percentage sets the threshold (default 70%)

Custom commands let viewers type something like !discord in chat and get an automatic response from the bot.

  1. Go to your channel Settings.
  2. Open the Commands tab.
  3. Type a trigger word (like discord, socials, schedule).
  4. Type the response the bot should send.
  5. Click Add Command.

The bot uses your chat prefix (default !) plus the trigger word. So if the trigger is discord, viewers type !discord.

Each channel has its own commands. You can edit or delete them anytime.

Timed messages are messages the bot sends automatically on a schedule during your stream. Useful for reminders like "Follow the channel!" or "Join the Discord" without you having to type them manually.

  1. Go to your channel Settings.
  2. Open the Timers tab.
  3. Add a message and set the interval (in minutes).
  4. Toggle it on.

The bot cycles through your timed messages during the stream. You can have multiple timed messages running at different intervals.

Integrations

3 articles

You can have Nexus send messages to a Discord channel when things happen, like going live or hitting a subscriber milestone.

Setting it up:

  1. In Discord, go to your server's Server Settings > Integrations > Webhooks.
  2. Click New Webhook, pick the channel you want notifications in, and copy the webhook URL.
  3. In Nexus, go to your channel Settings > Integrations tab.
  4. Paste the webhook URL.
  5. Toggle on the notification types you want (stream start, milestones).
  6. Click Send Test Message to make sure it works.

The test message sends a sample notification that looks exactly like what your viewers would see when you go live.

The webhook URL starts with https://discord.com/api/webhooks/. If yours looks different, double-check that you copied the right thing from Discord.

If you're switching from Nightbot, you can import your existing commands instead of recreating them one by one.

  1. In Nightbot, go to your Commands page and export or copy your commands.
  2. In Nexus, go to your channel Settings > Commands tab.
  3. Click Import from Nightbot.
  4. Paste your commands in the format: !command response text, one per line.
  5. Click Import.

Nexus reads each line, pulls out the trigger and response, and creates the commands for you. If a command already exists with the same trigger, it gets skipped.

I'm working on adding more integrations. These are planned but not ready yet:

  • Twitch - Link your Twitch channel and manage it from the same dashboard. Track followers, subs, and viewer stats alongside YouTube. Your bot commands, filters, and timers carry over.
  • Kick - Connect your Kick channel to Nexus. Same idea as Twitch: analytics, chat moderation, and custom commands across both platforms from one place.
  • Twitter/X - Auto-post to Twitter when you go live on YouTube. Nexus sends a tweet with your stream title and link so your followers know you're streaming.

No timeline on these yet. They'll show up in the Integrations tab of your channel settings as they become available.

Analytics & Data

4 articles

Click on any channel from your dashboard to open its analytics page. You'll see:

  • Current Stats - Subscriber count, total views, and total videos pulled from YouTube.
  • Growth Charts - How your subscribers and views have changed over the last 30 days.
  • Recent Videos - Your latest uploads with view counts, likes, and performance data.

The "Last Synced" timestamp shows when Nexus last pulled fresh data from YouTube.

Nexus takes daily snapshots of your channel's numbers and stores them so you can see trends over time. Tracked daily:

  • Subscriber count
  • Total view count
  • Total video count

This data helps you spot patterns. Maybe subscriber growth spikes after certain types of content, or views go up on specific days of the week.

Nexus keeps up to 24 months of analytics data per channel. That includes daily snapshots of subscribers, views, and video counts.

Data older than 24 months gets cleaned up automatically.

If you connect a channel today, tracking starts from that point forward. Nexus can't pull historical data from before you added the channel.

You can download all your Nexus data from the Settings page.

  1. Go to Settings > Data Management.
  2. Click Export All Data.
  3. You'll get a JSON file with your account info, channel data, commands, and growth history.

The export includes everything Nexus has stored for your account. You can open the JSON file in any text editor or import it into a spreadsheet.

Account & Privacy

4 articles

Your Nexus account is tied to your Google account. Profile name, email, and picture come from Google when you sign in.

From the Settings page you can:

  • See your connected Google account and linked channels
  • Change your theme (dark/light/system)
  • Adjust privacy settings (profile visibility, public stats)
  • Export or delete your data

To change your profile name or picture, update them on your Google account. The changes sync next time you log in.

Short version of how Nexus handles your data:

  • Nexus only accesses publicly available YouTube data (subscribers, views, etc.)
  • Your Google password is never stored. Authentication goes through Google OAuth.
  • Nexus does not read your private messages, DMs, or emails
  • Your data is not sold to anyone
  • Chat messages from your streams are not stored

Full details are in the Privacy Policy.

You can delete your account directly from the Settings page:

  1. Go to Settings > Data Management.
  2. Scroll to the bottom and click Delete Account.
  3. Confirm the deletion.

This permanently removes your account, all channel data, analytics history, bot settings, and commands. It cannot be undone.

You can also revoke Nexus's access to your Google account at any time from Google Account Permissions.
