Important: Bot Setup Requirements

You have to do this or the bot won't work. Add nexusbetabot as a Managing Moderator on your YouTube channel.

Here's how:
  • Open YouTube Studio
  • Go to Settings > Community
  • Under Moderators, add nexusbetabot
  • Give it Managing Moderator permissions
  • Hit Save
Without this, the bot can't send messages, delete spam, or do anything in your chat.
Short version: regular mod permissions aren't enough. The bot needs Managing Moderator to:
  • Send messages in chat
  • Delete spam and rule-breaking messages
  • Timeout and ban users
Without the highest role, some actions just silently fail. YouTube's API is picky about this.

Bot Identity and Configuration

The bot shows up in chat as the YouTube account it's logged into (currently nexusbetabot). Viewers see that account's name and profile picture when the bot responds.
The OAuth credentials on the server determine which YouTube account the bot acts as. Whatever YouTube channel those credentials belong to -- that's the bot. Messages appear under that channel's name. It's not a special "bot user," it's just a regular YouTube account with API access.
Not through Nexus. The bot's name in chat is whatever the YouTube account is called. To change it, you'd change the name on that YouTube channel directly.
Yes. Each channel has its own settings -- commands, moderation rules, timed messages, everything. Nothing is shared between channels unless you set them up the same way.
These work right away:

You can also make your own custom commands with whatever trigger and response you want.
Go to your channel settings, find the Commands section, and hit "Add Command." Pick a trigger (like !discord), type the response, and save. You can add cooldowns and restrict who can use it if you want.

What works for most people:
  • Start with medium spam sensitivity, adjust from there
  • Block links by default, whitelist your own socials
  • Caps filter at ~70% threshold
  • Turn on first-time chatter restrictions for busy streams
  • Set up auto-ban for obvious slurs/offensive stuff
  • Enable raid slow mode so your chat doesn't get flooded

Security and Privacy

No. You log in through Google directly and grant Nexus access. Your password never touches Nexus servers.
You click "Sign in with Google," get sent to Google's login page, approve the permissions, and Google gives Nexus a token. That token only works for the specific things you approved. You can revoke it anytime from your Google account settings.
Encrypted in the database. Never exposed in URLs, logs, or client-side code. Access tokens expire and refresh automatically. If you revoke access from Google, the tokens stop working immediately.
Yes. Everything's over HTTPS, database is encrypted, and OAuth tokens get extra encryption on top of that.
  • YouTube Read-Only - pulling your channel stats
  • YouTube Force SSL - encrypted API calls
  • YouTube Live Streaming - reading and sending chat messages
  • User Profile - showing your name/picture in the dashboard
That's it. No access to upload videos, delete content, or mess with your account settings.
Yep. Go to myaccount.google.com, find "Third-party apps with account access," find Nexus, and remove it. Done -- all tokens are immediately invalidated. You can also delete your Nexus account from the settings page.
Yes to both. Export everything from the settings page, or delete your account and all associated data permanently.

Troubleshooting

Most common fixes:
  • Did you add nexusbetabot as a Managing Moderator? This is the #1 reason it doesn't work.
  • Is the bot actually toggled on in your channel settings?
  • Is your stream live? The bot only works during active streams.
  • Check the status page -- something might be down.
  • Try toggling the bot off and on again.
  • Still broken? Re-authenticate your channel from the settings page.
